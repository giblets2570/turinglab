Teaching

We run after school and summer labs for primary aged children and their teachers.

Kids

By encouraging children to make short, fun and practical programmes, we supplement KS2 & KS3 maths and computing with real world skills such as design. A canonical example is teaching children about angles, length, functions and some basic algebra by showing them how to get a Robot Turtle to move around a table-top. Our Labs cover a number of text and non-text based languages, introduce several key concepts in maths, computing and design to get kids making fun new projects. Coding really is the new lego.

Teachers

70% of primary school teachers say they feel uncomfortable teaching the new computing syllabus that started in 2014. We want to change this, and show how computing can be both intuitive and magical at the same time. Our workshops cover an introduction to computer science history, help teachers to build their first programme and explain basic concepts along the way, and finally we introduce some tools for using in class.

Teaching Values

We believe that education should be inclusive, skills focused and driven by practical projects.

Inclusive

Turing Lab believes that a quality computer science education should be available to every child, not just a lucky few. We believe that learning to code is valuable, and possible, for everyone! This means that we work with children and teachers from all backgrounds and abilities, working with schools to ensure we provide an inclusive education for all.

Skills Focused

Turing Lab believes that education should be less about memorizing facts, or learning how to use particular tools and technologies. Practical

Turing Lab believes that you cannot learn computing or maths by simply reading about it. We teach the way we learn best; encouraging students to spend time practicing computer science by building things and engaging in discovery by pulling things apart. This encourages curiosity which we have seen motivates children into doing this themselves.
